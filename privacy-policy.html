This has been our approach and commitment for years and we remain to it. This policy explains to you how we gather information about you, what we do with it, who can access it and what you can do about it.

Thinkwik may also use your personally identifiable information to inform you of other services available and its affiliates. We also contact you via surveys to research your opinion of our services or of potential new services that we may be offering in the near future.

Thinkwik collects data and shares data with trusted partners to help perform statistical analysis, send you email or postal mail, provide customer support, or arrange for deliveries and we do not sell, rent or lease the customer information to third parties.

What we collect

We collect information for some legitimate purpose. Thinkwik will have information about you only if (a) you have provided the information yourself, (b) Thinkwik has automatically collected the information, or (c) We have obtained the information from a third party. When you contact us using an online web form, email us or enquire regarding our services, we will ask for information like your name, contact number, email address, project document, company name and country to process the inquiry form. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent and this will help feature on most browsers to provide information on how to accept cookies, disable cookies or notify you when to receive a new cookie.

We recommend you leave cookies turned on if you want to use some of the features of the service or else you can’t be able to access all services on the website.

Thinkwik uses Google Analytics to allow us to see how people interact with our website. For example, we can see that a certain service is really in-demand, or that one service is rarely used which will help us to improve and bring the highlight to that service and we provide it at our best.
